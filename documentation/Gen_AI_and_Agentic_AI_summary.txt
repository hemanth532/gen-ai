Day21:
Keyword search (dealing with facts) vs semantic search (intent, context, meaning)


🧠 What semantic search actually does
Understands the contextual meaning of words instead of literal matches.
Interprets user intent (e.g., informational vs. transactional queries).
Uses vector embeddings to represent text as numerical vectors so it can compare meaning, not just words. 
Leverages knowledge graphs (like Google’s Knowledge Graph) to understand entities and relationships. 
Considers context clues such as location, previous searches, and phrasing

Transformer is an AI model that looks at all words at the same time and uses self-attention to figure out which words matter most to each other.

🧩 Why Transformers were invented
Before transformers, models like RNNs read text one word at a time, which caused problems:
They forget earlier words in long sentences
They are slow because they process sequentially
They struggle with long-range relationships 
Transformers fix all of this by processing the entire sentence at once.

Workflow orchestration? 
Orchestration – someone should instruct which task be performed 
In an AI workflow, orchestration is the automated coordination and management of the entire end-to-end process of building, deploying, and maintaining AI models.
An AI pipeline is complex, involving many different tools, data sources, and computing environments. Orchestration acts as the "glue" that connects these disconnected steps into a single, automated, and repeatable loop.

Day27:
Orchestration
    └── Workflow
            └── Pipeline


Example in an AI application:
Pipeline: Retrieve documents → Rank results → Generate answer 
Workflow: Handle user query, approval checks, retries, notifications 
Orchestration: Coordinate multiple agents, workflows, databases, APIs, and monitoring across the entire platform 
A simple rule of thumb:
Pipeline = sequence of transformations 
Workflow = sequence of tasks with business logic 
Orchestration = coordination of workflows and systems
