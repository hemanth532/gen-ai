RAG combines retrieval of relevant documents with generative models to improve response quality. 
•	It uses a two-step process: first retrieving information, then generating a coherent answer. 
•	The retrieval component fetches contextually relevant data from a large corpus. 
•	The generative model synthesizes this information into a natural language response. 
•	RAG is particularly useful for tasks requiring up-to-date or specific knowledge. 
•	It helps mitigate the limitations of generative models by grounding responses in real data.
